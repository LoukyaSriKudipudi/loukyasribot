Response: The SUPTNIC-5 vaccine (also often referred to as EpiVacCorona or EpiVakCorona) is a COVID-19 vaccine developed in Russia by the Vector Institute (part of Rospotrebnadzor, the Russian Federal Service for Surveillance on Consumer Rights Protection and Human Well-being).

Here's a breakdown of key information about SUPTNIC-5:

‚Ä¢ Type: SUPTNIC-5 is a peptide-based subunit vaccine. This means it uses short fragments of proteins (peptides) from the SARS-CoV-2 virus, specifically designed to trigger an immune response. It does not contain the whole virus, live or inactivated.
‚Ä¢ Mechanism: The vaccine contains synthetic peptides corresponding to key antigens of the SARS-CoV-2 spike protein. When injected, these peptides are meant to prompt the immune system to recognize and fight off the actual virus if encountered.
‚Ä¢ Adjuvant: It uses an adjuvant to enhance the immune response. The specific adjuvant used in the final formulation is alum.

Development and Approval:
‚Ä¢ Developer: State Research Center of Virology and Biotechnology "Vector" (Vector Institute).
‚Ä¢ Speed of Development: Like many COVID-19 vaccines, SUPTNIC-5 underwent rapid development and clinical trials due to the global pandemic.
‚Ä¢ Emergency Use Authorization: It received emergency use authorization in Russia in October 2020, making it the second Russian COVID-19 vaccine to be approved, following Sputnik V.
‚Ä¢ Wider Rollout: It has been used in Russia's domestic vaccination program.

‚Ä¢ Two-Dose Regimen: Typically administered as two doses, with the second dose given a few weeks after the first.
‚Ä¢ Storage: Generally requires refrigeration, making it easier to store and distribute compared to some mRNA vaccines that need ultra-cold temperatures.
‚Ä¢ Target Audience: Approved for use in adults.
‚Ä¢ Efficacy Claims:
‚Ä¢ Vector Institute and Russian authorities have reported high efficacy rates from clinical trials, often in the range of 80-95%.
‚Ä¢ However, it's important to note that independent, peer-reviewed data on its efficacy, especially against emerging variants, has been less extensively published compared to some Western vaccines. Much of the available information comes from Russian government sources and the developer.
